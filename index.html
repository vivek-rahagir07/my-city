How They Compare

Traditional Traffic Lights

These lights operate on simple, fixed timers. They cycle through red, yellow, and green at predetermined intervals, regardless of the actual traffic.

  • Fixed Timers: Inefficient during off-peak hours or when traffic is one-sided.
  • Ignores Real-Time Data: Can't adapt to accidents, congestion, or special events.
  • No Priority: Cannot prioritize emergency vehicles, leading to critical delays.
  • Increased Idling: Leads to more frustrated drivers, wasted fuel, and higher emissions.

AI-Powered Traffic Lights

These "smart" lights use cameras, sensors, and AI algorithms to analyze traffic in real-time and make dynamic decisions.

  • Adaptive Control: Adjusts signal timing based on actual traffic flow, minimizing wait times.
  • Senses Demand: Can detect a long queue of cars and extend the green light.
  • Emergency Priority: Detects ambulances or police and clears their path.
  • Reduced Congestion: Improves overall traffic flow, leading to shorter commutes.
